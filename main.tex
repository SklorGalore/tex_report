\section{Converter Operation}

This project utilizes a full-bridge series resonant LLC converter with synchronous rectification.
The converter topology was chosen due to its ability to achieve soft switching under both switch turn on and switch turn off.
The converter can be seen below in figure \ref{fig:llcsr}.

\begin{figure}[h]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/LLCSR}
	\caption{The full-bridge LLC series resonant converter with synchronous rectifier.}
	\label{fig:llcsr}
\end{figure}

This converter uses eight switches operating in four pairs of two complementary switches, two pairs in the high side inverter and two pairs in the low side rectifier.
In addition, these switches operate synchronously across the inverter and rectifier meaning that although there are eight switches, there are only two distinct physical states.


\subsection{Switching Modes}

\begin{figure}[h]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/mode1}
	\caption{The first switching state with switches 1, 4, 5, and 8 on.}
	\label{fig:mode1}
\end{figure}

During the first switching mode, the first two pairs of switches, 1, 4, 5, and 8, are on generating the positive portion of the square wave from the source and charging the resonant tank which is resonating at the primary resonant frequency $f_{r1}$.
This physical state exists until a time $t_1$ at which the current switches are opened, and the other switches are closed, following a dead time $t_1\rightarrow t_2$.

\begin{figure}[h]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/mode2b}
	\caption{The second switching state with switches 2, 3, 6, and 7 on.}
	\label{fig:mode2b}
\end{figure}

During this stage, a similar but mirrored process occurs where the second two pairs of switches, 2, 3, 6, 7, are generating the negative portion of the square wave from the source and sending energy through the resonant tank and to the low side rectifier.
This state will exist until a time $t_3$ at which this combination of switches will be opened and a dead time will exist until the cycle resets at the end of the switching period.
In order to achieve soft switching through zero current and zero voltage switching methods, the LLC resonant tank and transformer are utilized by choosing a switching frequency to achieve a desired outcome.
