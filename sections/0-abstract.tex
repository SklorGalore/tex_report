\begin{abstract}
    The design of LLC resonant converters typically relies on iterative analytical methods and time-domain simulations, which become computationally expensive when exploring large design spaces. This paper proposes a data-driven framework for rapid, efficiency-oriented design of LLC resonant converters. A comprehensive dataset is generated through large-scale time-domain simulations, covering a wide range of operating conditions, including input and output voltages, switching frequency, load, and resonant tank parameters. Based on this dataset, a Random Forest regression model is trained to predict converter efficiency. The proposed model achieves high accuracy, with a prediction error below 1\% compared to simulation results. The framework is validated through time-domain simulations, showing strong agreement between predicted and actual converter performance. The proposed approach enables fast exploration of the design space, allowing efficient identification of optimal component values and operating conditions without requiring extensive iterative simulations.
\end{abstract}
