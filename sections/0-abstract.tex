\begin{abstract}
    The design of LLC resonant converters typically relies on iterative analytical methods and time-domain simulations, which become computationally expensive when exploring large design spaces. This paper proposes a data-driven framework for rapid, efficiency-oriented design of LLC resonant converters. A comprehensive dataset is generated through large-scale time-domain simulations, covering a wide range of operating conditions, including input and output voltages, switching frequency, load, and resonant tank parameters.Based on this dataset, a machine learning model is trained to predict converter efficiency using a Random Forest regression approach. The model demonstrates high accuracy, with a prediction error below 1\% compared to simulation results. The proposed framework is validated through simulation, showing that the predicted efficiency closely matches the actual performance of the designed converter.This approach enables fast exploration of the design space, allowing designers to efficiently identify optimal component values and operating conditions for high-efficiency LLC resonant converters without requiring extensive iterative simulations.
\end{abstract}
