\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:Conclusion}

This work presented a data-driven methodology for the design and efficiency evaluation of LLC resonant converters. A comprehensive dataset was generated through time-domain simulations covering a wide range of operating conditions, and relevant features were extracted to train machine learning models.

Among the evaluated approaches, the Random Forest model demonstrated high accuracy in predicting converter efficiency, enabling fast and reliable exploration of the design space without the need for exhaustive simulations. The proposed framework combines physical insight with data-driven modeling, allowing efficient identification of operating regions with high performance.

Simulation results confirmed stable operation near the resonant frequency, reduced switching losses, and high efficiency levels, validating both the converter design and the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Overall, the method provides a practical and computationally efficient tool for the design and optimization of LLC converters, with potential applicability to other resonant power converter topologies.
