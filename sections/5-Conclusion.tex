\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:Conclusion}

This work presented a data-driven methodology for the design and efficiency evaluation of LLC resonant converters. A comprehensive dataset was generated through time-domain simulations over a wide range of operating conditions, and relevant features were extracted to train machine learning models.

Among the evaluated approaches, the Random Forest model provided the best performance, achieving $R^2 = 0.9959$, RMSE = 0.0125, and an average prediction error of 0.78\%. These results show that the model can accurately capture the relationship between operating conditions and converter efficiency, enabling fast exploration of the design space without relying on iterative time-domain simulations.

The proposed framework combines data-driven modeling with physical design considerations, allowing the identification of high-efficiency operating points while maintaining near-resonant operation and proper impedance matching.

Simulation results confirmed stable operation, reduced switching losses, and high efficiency levels, supporting both the validity of the design and the accuracy of the proposed approach.

Overall, the methodology provides an efficient way to explore the design space of LLC converters and can be extended to other resonant converter topologies within the range of operating conditions used for training. This approach is limited to the operating conditions covered by the training dataset, and future work will address model generalization under wider operating ranges and experimental validation.
